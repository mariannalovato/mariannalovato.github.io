Undergraduate tutoring

Since the start of my PhD, I have been tutoring undergraduate modules offered at the School of Politics and International Relations.
As part of my teaching responsibilities, I prepare and deliver 50-minute tutorial sessions in line with the module coordinator’s instructions, I hold regular office hours, correspond with students and grade midterm essays as well as final exams.

Following the Harvard Kennedy School case study method, I designed a case study to be used for undergraduate and graduate level courses on EU foreign policy.

The case study focuses on the role of the COREU network in Common Foreign and Security Policy/Common Security and Defense Policy decision-making.

The case study, which comes with a detailed teaching plan, is free and open-access. I was awarded a certificate attesting to my skills in case study writing.
